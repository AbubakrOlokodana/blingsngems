FAQs

What is the difference between white gold and yellow gold?

The main difference between the white gold and yellow gold is the color. While the white gold is mixed with white metals like nickel, yellow gold is mixed with yellow metals like copper. However, the cost of white gold and yellow gold is relatively the same as they are both made of gold and other alloy metals.

What are the four C's of diamonds?

The 4 C’s of diamond are carat,cut, clarity and color which are used to determine the quality and value of a diamond. These features are always examined when we need to distinguish the quality of one diamond from another.

What does a gia certification for diamond or colored stone mean?

GIA stands for Gemological Institute of America and they are the foremost authority in diamond and colored stone grading. If you want the best, purchase diamonds that come with a diamond/color stone grading report. GIA is a laboratory that adheres to a strict standard of diamond grading. As with all fine jewelry purchases, it gives our customers confidence that the diamond they are buying is truly the color, cut, and clarity that they intend to buy.

Is it a non-tarnish piece?

Our pure gold, stainless and other gemstones doesn't tarnish or turn color. But the metals (like silver, copper, and nickel) that are mixed with gold oxidizes when it's exposed to air, sweat, makeup, and other chemicals hence why we provide after care and instructions on how to clean and care for your jewelries.
